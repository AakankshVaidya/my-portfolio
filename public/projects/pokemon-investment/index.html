The Grading Effect

How a number from 1–10 can multiply a card's value by 50×

Professional grading services like PSA, BGS, and CGC authenticate and rate a card's condition on a 1–10 scale. A card graded PSA 10 (Gem Mint) can be worth 5–50× more than the same card ungraded.

This is where Pokémon cards become a real asset class. Grading creates verifiable, tiered value — much like a diamond's certification.

So... is it a valid investment?

The Upside

Some cards have dramatically outperformed the S&P 500 and even NVIDIA over the last 5 years.

Real Asset Value

Professional grading creates verifiable, tiered value. A PSA 10 Charizard is worth 30× more than ungraded. Condition is everything.

The Volatility

Cards can lose 50%+ in months during market corrections. The Shadowless Charizard dropped from $5,000 to $765 in under a year. This is not a stable asset.

The Liquidity Risk

You can't sell a card instantly like a stock. Finding a buyer at fair value takes time, and fees from grading and marketplace commissions eat into returns.

The Bottom Line

The best investment is the one you never want to sell.
